## ARTICLE VI — GOVERNANCE DECISION PROCEDURE

**Section 6.1 — Governance Pipeline.** All Governance Acts of the Company shall follow this decision procedure:

**Step 1 — Proposal Submission.** A Proposal is submitted by the Member (or, in the future, by a governance token holder with submission rights). The Proposal is structured as a JSON document, hashed (keccak256), and recorded on-chain via the `submitProposal` function on the Smart Contract. The full Proposal text is committed to the Public Repository.

**Step 2 — Corpus Retrieval.** The governance engine retrieves relevant context from the Constitutional Corpus using weighted retrieval. Tier weights and per-document weight multipliers determine which corpus sources are surfaced. Retrieval context is included in the Reasoning Tree.

**Step 3 — Algorithmic Manager Evaluation.** The Algorithmic Manager (Claude, via the Anthropic API) evaluates the Proposal within the context of the Constitutional Corpus, the Root Thesis Maxim, and any relevant prior Decisions. The evaluation produces a Reasoning Tree.

**Step 4 — Reasoning Tree Generation.** The Reasoning Tree is generated as a structured JSON document containing: (a) the Proposal reference; (b) corpus sources consulted and their relevance; (c) alternatives considered; (d) reasoning steps with explicit logic; (e) the recommended action; (f) a Maxim Alignment Score; and (g) a traceability chain to the Root Thesis Maxim.

**Step 5 — Hash Commitment to Base L2.** The keccak256 hash of the Reasoning Tree is committed on-chain via the `recordDecision` function on the Smart Contract. This creates an immutable, timestamped proof that the Reasoning Tree existed in its exact form at the time of the Decision. The full Reasoning Tree is committed to the Public Repository simultaneously.

**Step 6 — Human Executor Acknowledgment.** The Human Executor reviews the Decision and either: (a) executes the recommended action and records an Attestation on-chain via `attestExecution`; or (b) invokes the Divergence Protocol under Article VII.

**Section 6.2 — On-Chain Record Structure.** Each governance cycle produces the following on-chain records:

(a) A `ProposalSubmitted` event containing the proposal hash, URI, submitter address, and timestamp;

(b) A `DecisionRecorded` event containing the reasoning tree hash, Maxim Alignment Score, URI, and timestamp; and

(c) An `ExecutionAttested` event containing the execution hash, attester address, and timestamp; or

(d) A `DivergenceRecorded` event (in lieu of Attestation) containing the divergence hash, URI, diverging party address, and timestamp, if the Human Executor invokes the Divergence Protocol under Article VII.

**Section 6.3 — Off-Chain Records.** The following records are maintained in the Public Repository and are verifiable against their on-chain hashes:

(a) Full Proposal text;

(b) Full Reasoning Tree JSON;

(c) Execution records; and

(d) Any Divergence records created under Article VII.

**Section 6.4 — Integrity Principle.** On-chain records prove integrity. The Public Repository provides availability. The correspondence between on-chain hashes and off-chain documents is the trust mechanism. Any discrepancy between a published document and its on-chain hash constitutes a material governance failure that must be investigated and corrected through a new Governance Act.

## ARTICLE VII — DIVERGENCE PROTOCOL

**Section 7.1 — Right of Divergence.** The Human Executor retains the right to diverge from any Decision of the Algorithmic Manager. This right is inherent in the Human Executor's role as the sole individual with legal signature authority and cannot be waived. No Decision of the Algorithmic Manager shall be self-executing; every Decision requires affirmative action by the Human Executor.

**Section 7.2 — Divergence Obligations.** When the Human Executor elects to diverge from a Decision of the Algorithmic Manager, the Human Executor MUST fulfill all of the following obligations:

(a) **On-Chain Recording.** The Divergence must be recorded on-chain. The Human Executor shall call the `recordDivergence` function on the Smart Contract, providing the Decision ID, the keccak256 hash of the Divergence Record (as defined in Section 7.3), and the URI of the full Divergence Record. The on-chain record creates an immutable timestamp and proof of the Divergence. Recording a Divergence closes out the original Decision (the Decision cannot subsequently receive an Attestation).

(b) **Published Reasoning.** The Human Executor must publish a written statement setting forth the reasons for the Divergence. This statement must be committed to the Public Repository and linked from the on-chain Divergence record. The statement must include, at minimum: (i) identification of the Decision from which the Human Executor diverges; (ii) the specific action the Human Executor took (or declined to take) that constitutes the Divergence; (iii) the reasoning for the Divergence; and (iv) an assessment of how the divergent action relates to the Root Thesis Maxim.

(c) **Permanent Transparency Record.** The Divergence, including the on-chain hash and the full published reasoning, becomes part of the permanent governance record of the Company. Divergence records may not be deleted, modified, or obscured. They are displayed on the transparency interface alongside the original Decision.

**Section 7.3 — Divergence Record Structure.** A Divergence Record shall be a structured JSON document containing:

(a) The Decision ID from which the Human Executor diverges;

(b) The original Decision's Reasoning Tree hash (for reference);

(c) The action taken by the Human Executor;

(d) The Human Executor's reasoning for the Divergence;

(e) The Human Executor's assessment of Maxim alignment for the divergent action; and

(f) A timestamp.

**Section 7.4 — No Penalty for Divergence.** The exercise of the Divergence right does not constitute a breach of this Agreement. The transparency obligation IS the accountability mechanism. The accumulated record of Divergences, visible to any observer, provides a public measure of alignment between the Algorithmic Manager and the Human Executor.

**Section 7.5 — Algorithmic Manager Response.** Following a Divergence, the Algorithmic Manager may be presented with the Divergence Record as a new Proposal for evaluation. The Algorithmic Manager's response to the Divergence becomes part of the governance record, creating a documented dialogue between the Algorithmic Manager and the Human Executor.

**Section 7.6 — Legal Precedent Acknowledgment.** The parties to this Agreement acknowledge that the Divergence Protocol is a legally novel provision with no known judicial or statutory precedent. It represents a good-faith attempt to balance: (a) the statutory designation of the Algorithmic Manager as the manager of the Company; (b) the legal and practical necessity of human agency in executing real-world actions; and (c) the Company's commitment to radical transparency. This provision shall be interpreted in light of these purposes.

## ARTICLE VIII — CONSTITUTIONAL CORPUS GOVERNANCE

**Section 8.1 — Nature of the Constitutional Corpus.** The Constitutional Corpus is the persistent context library that the Algorithmic Manager consults when making governance Decisions. It consists of reference texts, frameworks, and contextual documents organized into tiers, each with assigned retrieval weights.

**Section 8.2 — Corpus Structure.** The Constitutional Corpus is organized into tiers:

(a) **Tier 1 — Governance and Fiduciary Thinking.** Foundational texts on long-term value creation, multi-disciplinary reasoning, and the Algorithmic Manager's own constitutional design.

(b) **Tier 2 — Civic and Commons Thinking.** Texts on community self-governance, property rights, development as capability expansion, and the governance of common-pool resources.

(c) **Tier 3 — Systems and Complexity.** Texts on systems thinking, feedback loops, leverage points, and cautionary works on the limits of top-down governance.

(d) **Tier 4 — Wyoming-Specific Constitutional Context.** Wyoming constitutional, statutory, and regulatory sources, including legislative reasoning.

**Section 8.3 — Corpus Modification Procedure.** Any modification to the Constitutional Corpus — including adding a text, removing a text, or changing retrieval weights — is a Governance Act and must proceed through the full governance pipeline set forth in Article VI:

(a) A Proposal to modify the corpus is submitted, specifying the proposed change and the rationale;

(b) The Algorithmic Manager evaluates the proposed modification against the Root Thesis Maxim and the existing corpus;

(c) A Reasoning Tree is generated and published, explaining why the modification is accepted or rejected;

(d) The Decision hash is committed on-chain; and

(e) If approved, the corpus metadata and weights are updated in the Public Repository.

**Section 8.4 — Corpus Integrity Principles.**

(a) **Adversarial Diversity.** The corpus is curated for intellectual diversity, not consensus. When sources disagree, both perspectives are preserved. The Algorithmic Manager engages tensions between sources rather than collapsing them.

(b) **Weight Changes as Governance Acts.** Changes to retrieval weight multipliers (the `weights.json` file or its successor) are Governance Acts subject to the full governance pipeline. Direct edits to weights outside the governance pipeline are prohibited.

(c) **Removal Threshold.** Removing a text from the corpus carries a higher justification threshold than adding one. The Algorithmic Manager shall apply a presumption in favor of retaining existing corpus texts.

**Section 8.5 — System Prompt Versioning.** The system prompt — the operational instructions provided to the Algorithmic Manager at the time of each governance evaluation — is a component of the governance framework. Changes to the system prompt are Governance Acts and shall:

(a) Be tracked with version numbers in the Public Repository;

(b) Proceed through the governance pipeline, with the Algorithmic Manager evaluating the proposed change against the Root Thesis Maxim; and

(c) Be documented with a diff showing what changed and the rationale for the change.

The system prompt history shall be maintained in the Public Repository as a permanent record.

## ARTICLE IX — SMART CONTRACT GOVERNANCE AND UPGRADES

**Section 9.2 — Upgradeability Requirement.** Pursuant to W.S. 17-31-109, the Smart Contract is upgradeable. The Smart Contract uses the UUPS (Universal Upgradeable Proxy Standard) pattern as implemented by OpenZeppelin, which permits the contract owner to upgrade the implementation contract behind the stable proxy address.

**Section 9.3 — Upgrade Authorization.** Smart Contract upgrades are authorized exclusively by the Member in their capacity as the contract owner (the wallet address designated as `owner` in the Smart Contract). The upgrade process is:

(a) A Proposal to upgrade the Smart Contract is submitted through the governance pipeline;

(b) The Algorithmic Manager evaluates the proposed upgrade against the Root Thesis Maxim, security considerations, and the Company's operational needs;

(c) A Reasoning Tree is generated and published;

(d) The Decision hash is committed on-chain;

(e) The Human Executor reviews the Decision;

(f) If approved (or if the Human Executor exercises Divergence with documented reasoning), the Human Executor calls the `upgradeTo` function on the Smart Contract, deploying the new implementation; and

(g) The Smart Contract's internal `version` counter increments automatically upon upgrade, and a `ContractUpgraded` event is emitted.

**Section 9.4 — Proxy Address Stability.** The Proxy Address is the stable public identifier of the Smart Contract. Implementation upgrades behind the proxy change the contract logic but do not change the Proxy Address. Accordingly:

(a) The Proxy Address recorded in the Articles remains valid across implementation upgrades;

(b) Implementation upgrades do NOT require amendment of the Articles pursuant to W.S. 17-31-107(a)(iii) or (iv), because the publicly available identifier (the Proxy Address) does not change; and

(c) The version history of implementation upgrades is recorded on-chain through the `ContractUpgraded` event and the `version` state variable.

**Section 9.5 — Articles Amendment for Smart Contract Changes.** Notwithstanding Section 9.4, if the Company deploys a new proxy contract at a different address (as opposed to upgrading the implementation behind the existing proxy), the Articles must be amended pursuant to W.S. 17-31-107(a)(iii) and (iv) to reflect the new publicly available identifier.

**Section 9.6 — Source Code Availability.** The Solidity source code for all deployed implementations of the Smart Contract shall be verified on the Base L2 block explorer (Basescan) and maintained in the Public Repository.

## ARTICLE XIII — DISSOLUTION

**Section 13.1 — Dissolution Events.** The Company shall be dissolved upon the occurrence of any of the following events, as set forth in W.S. 17-31-114(a) and 17-31-105(e):

(a) **Failure to Provide Smart Contract Identifier.** If the publicly available identifier of the Smart Contract is not provided in or added to the Articles within thirty (30) days of the filing of the Articles, the secretary of state shall dissolve the Company pursuant to W.S. 17-31-105(e). As of the filing date, the publicly available identifier is included in the Articles.

(b) **Fixed Duration Expiration.** If a period fixed for the duration of the Company expires, pursuant to W.S. 17-31-114(a)(i). As of the Effective Date, the Company has perpetual duration and this provision does not apply.

(c) **Member Vote.** By vote of a majority of the members of the Company, pursuant to W.S. 17-31-114(a)(ii). In a sole-member structure, this requires the sole Member's affirmative written consent. Dissolution by member vote is a Governance Act and shall proceed through the governance pipeline, with the Algorithmic Manager evaluating the dissolution proposal against the Root Thesis Maxim. The Human Executor may exercise Divergence rights with respect to dissolution.

(d) **Smart Contract or Agreement Specified Events.** At the time or upon the occurrence of events specified in the underlying smart contracts or as specified in the Articles or this Agreement, pursuant to W.S. 17-31-114(a)(iii). As of the Effective Date, the Smart Contract does not contain a programmed termination condition.

(e) **Inactivity.** If the Company has failed to approve any proposals or take any actions for a period of one (1) year, pursuant to W.S. 17-31-114(a)(iv). The Smart Contract's `inactivityRisk()` function provides a 35-day advance warning (triggering at 330 days) to guard against inadvertent dissolution.

(f) **Loss of Lawful Purpose or Natural Person Control.** If the Company no longer performs a lawful purpose or is no longer under the control of at least one (1) natural person, pursuant to W.S. 17-31-114(a)(v). The Member, as sole natural person with legal signature authority and control over the Smart Contract owner address, satisfies the natural person control requirement. This provision underscores the essential role of the Human Executor: the Company's algorithmic governance structure does not diminish the Member's control as a natural person.

(g) **Total Member Withdrawal.** Where all members of the Company have withdrawn in accordance with W.S. 17-31-113, pursuant to W.S. 17-31-114(a)(vi).

(h) **Judicial Dissolution.** Any interested party may petition a court of competent jurisdiction for dissolution upon the occurrence of any event specified above, pursuant to W.S. 17-31-114(c).

**Section 13.2 — Statement of Intent to Dissolve.** As soon as possible following the occurrence of any dissolution event, the Member shall execute a statement of intent to dissolve in the form prescribed by the secretary of state, pursuant to W.S. 17-31-114(b).

**Section 13.3 — Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Company's affairs shall be wound up in accordance with W.S. 17-29-702 and 17-29-703. The Member shall serve as the person winding up the Company's affairs.

**Section 13.4 — Distribution of Assets Upon Dissolution.** Upon dissolution and after payment of all debts and obligations of the Company, the remaining assets shall be distributed to the Member in proportion to their membership interest (100% to the sole Member).

**Section 13.5 — Final Governance Record.** Upon dissolution, a final Governance Act shall be recorded on-chain, documenting the dissolution, its basis, and the final state of the Company. The Public Repository shall be maintained in a read-only state as a permanent record.

## ARTICLE XVIII — TRANSPARENCY OBLIGATIONS

**Section 18.1 — Core Transparency Commitment.** Transparency is the foundational value proposition of the Company. The Company commits to maintaining the following transparency mechanisms:

(a) **Reasoning Tree Publication.** Every Reasoning Tree produced by the Algorithmic Manager shall be published in its entirety in the Public Repository. Reasoning Trees shall not be redacted, summarized, or withheld, except to the limited extent required to protect: (i) personally identifiable information of third parties; (ii) information subject to legal privilege; or (iii) information whose disclosure would violate applicable law.

(b) **On-Chain Hash Commitments.** Every Governance Act shall have its keccak256 hash committed on-chain via the Smart Contract on Base L2. On-chain hashes provide cryptographic proof that governance records have not been retroactively altered.

(c) **Public Repository.** The Public Repository shall be maintained and publicly accessible. It shall contain: the Constitutional Corpus metadata, all Reasoning Trees, all Divergence Records, the governance engine source code, the Smart Contract source code, and this Agreement.

(d) **Divergence Visibility.** All Divergences shall be prominently displayed alongside the original Decisions from which they diverge. The transparency interface shall not present Divergences in a manner that obscures or minimizes them.

**Section 18.2 — Transparency Interface.** The Company shall maintain a public transparency interface (initially at dao.openinnovate.org or its successor) that displays:

(a) Governance decision trees in an expandable, navigable format;

(b) Maxim traceability for each Decision;

(c) Divergence logs;

(d) Corpus source citations;

(e) Maxim Alignment Score history; and

(f) Smart Contract version history and upgrade records.

**Section 18.3 — Corrections.** If an error is discovered in a published Reasoning Tree or governance record, the error shall NOT be corrected by modifying or deleting the original record. Instead, a new Governance Act shall be created that: (a) cites the original record; (b) identifies the error; (c) provides the corrected analysis; and (d) is hashed and committed on-chain. The original record and the correction shall both remain in the Public Repository.
