### DECISION DOMAIN 1: Business Banking

**Question:** Which business bank account should OpenInnovate DAO LLC open as its primary operating account?

**Options for evaluation:**

**A. Mercury**
- No minimum balance, no monthly fees
- Designed for startups and tech companies
- API access for programmatic transaction queries
- Integrates with accounting tools (QuickBooks, Xero)
- FDIC insured via partner banks (up to $5M through sweep network)
- Virtual and physical debit cards
- Treasury product for idle cash (future relevance)
- DAO/crypto-adjacent companies are known customers

**B. Relay**
- No minimum balance, no monthly fees
- Multiple checking accounts for budgeting/allocation
- Profit First methodology built in
- FDIC insured
- Simpler interface, fewer integrations
- No API access

**C. Bluevine**
- No minimum balance, no monthly fees
- Earns interest on deposits (currently ~2-4% on balances up to $250K)
- FDIC insured
- Line of credit product available (future relevance)
- Less startup-focused, more general SMB

**D. Traditional bank (Chase Business, US Bank, etc.)**
- Physical branch access
- Broader lending relationships
- Often has monthly fees or minimum balance requirements
- Slower onboarding, more documentation

**Evaluation criteria:** Alignment with transparency thesis (API/data access), cost (must be $0/month), DAO-friendliness (will they close a DAO LLC account?), integration potential with governance engine, path to future financial products.

### DECISION DOMAIN 2: D-U-N-S Number & Business Credit Foundation

**Question:** Should OpenInnovate pursue expedited ($50) or standard (free, ~30 days) D-U-N-S registration, and what is the optimal sequencing for establishing the business credit profile?

**Options for evaluation:**

**A. Expedited registration (~$50, ~5-7 business days)**
- Starts the credit clock sooner
- Enables tradeline applications within 1-2 weeks
- Small upfront cost but accelerates entire credit timeline by ~3 weeks

**B. Standard registration (free, ~30 days)**
- No cost
- Delays tradeline applications and entire credit-building timeline by ~3 weeks
- Aligns with lower-risk, lower-cost operating philosophy

**Evaluation criteria:** Time value of earlier credit building vs. cost consciousness. Does the 3-week acceleration compound meaningfully over a 12-month horizon?

### DECISION DOMAIN 6: Sixt Business Account & Vehicle Strategy

**Question:** Should OpenInnovate register for a Sixt Business account to access corporate vehicle rental/subscription pricing, and under what conditions would a Sixt+ subscription be financially justified?

**Facts established:**
- Sixt Business registration is free, requires only business name, EIN, and address
- Corporate customers receive up to 15% discount on subscriptions and negotiated rental rates
- Sixt+ subscription starts at ~$649-719/month (consumer), ~$552-611/month with business discount
- Monthly rate includes insurance, taxes, maintenance, registration, roadside assistance
- $199 one-time enrollment fee
- 30-day minimum, cancel anytime after
- Vehicle costs are not a business deduction unless used for documented business purposes
- Business-use mileage/percentage is deductible on Schedule C

**Options for evaluation:**

**A. Register business account now, subscribe later**
- $0 cost to register
- Locks in corporate pricing tier
- Subscribe only when/if the member needs monthly vehicle access
- No commitment, pure optionality

**B. Register and subscribe when vehicle needed**
- Use corporate discount to reduce monthly cost vs. consumer pricing
- Track business-use mileage for partial Schedule C deduction
- Compare total cost against alternatives (car ownership, lease, standard rental)

**C. Do not pursue**
- Vehicle rental is not core to DAO operations
- Distraction from higher-priority financial infrastructure

**Evaluation criteria:** Cost-benefit of registration (free), strategic value of corporate pricing access, alignment with maxim (does vehicle access compound the member's operational agency?).

### DECISION DOMAIN 7: Tax Optimization & Deduction Strategy

**Question:** What is the optimal 2026 Schedule C deduction strategy that maximizes legitimate tax recovery while maintaining clean audit posture?

**Parameters:**
- LLC is single-member, disregarded entity (Schedule C)
- Wyoming: no state income tax
- LLC currently has $0 revenue
- IRS hobby loss rule (Section 183): must show profit intent, ideally profit in 3 of 5 years
- Home office deduction may not be available (shared workspace constraints)

**Request:** Recommend a target deduction strategy, categorized by expense type, with risk assessment for each category. Identify which deductions are defensible, which are aggressive, and which should be avoided. Propose a timeline for generating token revenue to establish profit intent before the hobby loss window becomes a concern.
