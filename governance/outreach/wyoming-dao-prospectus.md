## Landscape Overview

Wyoming became the first US state to recognize DAOs as LLCs on July 1, 2021 (SF0038, codified as W.S. 17-31). As of March 2023, **800+ entities** with "DAO" in their name existed in Wyoming's LLC registry. Not all are compliant or operational. Many are shell filings, dormant, or dissolved under the 1-year inactivity rule.

On July 1, 2024, Wyoming added the **DUNA** (Decentralized Unincorporated Nonprofit Association) framework, designed for DAOs seeking legal standing while maintaining decentralization. Requires 100+ members.

### Key Wyoming DAO Requirements
- Name must include "DAO" or "LAO" + LLC designation
- Smart contract identifier filed within 30 days or entity dissolves
- Member-managed or algorithmically managed (no manager-managed option)
- Auto-dissolves after 1 year of no proposals/actions
- Annual report: $60/year
- Initial filing: $100

### Known Criticisms (Gabriel Shapiro, LexDAO analyses)
- No actual blockchain/decentralization requirement — any LLC can claim "DAO" status
- Member-managed requirement prevents foundation-governed development phases
- "Algorithmically managed" must be upgradeable — contradicts blockchain immutability
- Naming restrictions limit branding flexibility
- Registered agent mandate contradicts borderless ethos
- 1-year inactivity dissolution penalizes stable projects

## The 800+ Unnamed Entities

The Wyoming SOS database (wyobiz.wyo.gov) is CAPTCHA-protected and doesn't support programmatic entity-type filtering. The 800+ figure (as of March 2023) includes:

**Estimated breakdown:**
- **Shell filings** (~60-70%): Entities filed for the novelty, registered agent income, or speculative purposes. Many lack smart contract filings and auto-dissolved after 30 days or 1 year.
- **Dormant/dissolved** (~20-25%): Filed with intent but failed to maintain annual reports or approve proposals within 1 year.
- **Operational** (~5-10%): Entities with active governance, treasury, and operations.

The 1-year inactivity dissolution rule means the survival rate is inherently low. Most of the 800+ entities are likely dissolved or inactive by 2026.

### How to Find Active Targets

1. **Wyoming SOS manual search** — Search wyobiz.wyo.gov for "DAO" entities, filter by active status. Requires manual CAPTCHA completion.
2. **Wyoming annual report database** — Entities that paid their 2025/2026 annual report are active. SOS phone line (307.777.7311) may provide aggregate data.
3. **Cross-reference with known projects** — Search GitHub, Twitter, governance forums for projects that mention Wyoming DAO LLC status.
4. **Registered agent databases** — Northwest Registered Agent, Mountain Business Center, and Wyoming Agent serve many DAO LLCs. They may aggregate client lists.
