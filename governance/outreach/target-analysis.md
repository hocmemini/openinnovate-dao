## Tier A: High-Fit Targets (strong alignment, clear value prop)

### 1. LexDAO

**What they are:** Legal engineering guild. Wyoming unincorporated nonprofit (501(c)(6)). ~4,316 Discord members. Building tools at law + blockchain intersection. Actively transitioning to Wyoming DUNA legal structure.

**Current governance:** Operator (Tertius/Nick Viavant) + Assistant Operator (Cimply) + elected committees. "Do-ocracy" and "lazy consensus" decision-making. Bi-weekly governance meetings on Discord (Wed 12pm EST). Snapshot voting.

- They're transitioning to Wyoming DUNA — we have the deepest Wyoming DAO legal corpus (19 documents, 1.2x weight)
- They understand the concept immediately (legal engineers doing legal engineering)
- An audit of their own governance is both useful to them and a credibility play for us

**Recommended service:** Governance Health Check (free) → Governance Audit ($1,500)
**Value prop:** "You're a legal engineering guild with undefined governance procedures. We built an AI that evaluates governance decisions against a 155-document constitutional corpus — including the Wyoming statutes you're transitioning to. Let us run your own governance through it."

**Contact:** Discord (lexdao.chat), reach Tertius or drllau. Twitter @lex_DAO.

**Outreach priority:** #1 (highest fit, shared legal-engineering DNA)

### 2. CityDAO

**What they are:** Wyoming DAO LLC that purchased 40 acres in Park County, WY. First DAO to own land. Founded by Scott Fitsimones. Citizenship NFTs for governance participation.

**Current governance:** Has gone through 4 iterations: Core Team → Council → Guilds → Projects-based. 8-person multisig (4/8 threshold). Snapshot voting.

**Governance problems (documented):**
- Governance structure became "a blocker to understanding what the governance process should be"
- Growing unease and frustration among members
- Guild overlap caused confusion about responsibilities
- Lack of centralized management meant tasks weren't aligned toward objectives
- Autonomous guilds moved at different paces, creating inefficiency
- Well-documented in a Harvard Kennedy School case study

**Why OpenInnovate matters to them:**
- Fellow Wyoming DAO LLC — shared legal framework, we have the corpus
- Their governance problems are exactly what our engine evaluates
- 4 governance iterations in ~4 years = they haven't found what works
- A governance audit provides an external, structured assessment they've never had
- The Harvard case study means they're self-aware about their governance challenges

**Recommended service:** Governance Audit ($1,500-$3,000)
**Value prop:** "You've been through 4 governance models in 4 years. Our engine evaluates governance decisions against Ostrom's commons principles, democratic theory, and the same Wyoming DAO statutes we both operate under. Here's what that analysis looks like when we run it on ourselves [link to demo report]. Want to see what it says about CityDAO?"

**Contact:** Their Discord, or directly to Scott Fitsimones (TED speaker, active on Twitter)

**Outreach priority:** #2 (shared Wyoming LLC, documented governance pain, compelling case study potential)

**Why OpenInnovate matters to them:**
- Their governance complexity is exactly what our engine is designed to evaluate
- They've struggled publicly with governance quality — an external audit provides independent assessment
- A Gitcoin governance audit would be HIGHLY visible (they're a household name in web3)
- Even if they don't pay, a published analysis of their governance generates massive credibility

**Recommended service:** Governance Audit ($3,000-$5,000) or Published Research (free — brand play)
**Value prop:** "Gitcoin has distributed hundreds of millions through governance. We built an AI that evaluates governance quality against a constitutional corpus covering Ostrom's commons principles, democratic theory, and systems thinking. We'd like to run your governance framework through it — either as a paid engagement or as published research with your input."

**Contact:** Gitcoin governance forum (gov.gitcoin.co), Steward Council members, Discord

**Outreach priority:** #3 (high visibility, real governance pain, but harder to convert to paid)

## What We Run Through the System

**Health Check:** Nothing through the pipeline. Manual assessment using the health check interview guide + Ostrom's principles checklist.

**Full Audit:**
1. Curate a temporary corpus from their governance documents (their constitution = their tier-1)
2. Our existing corpus tiers supplement as applicable (Wyoming law for Wyoming DAOs, Ostrom for all, etc.)
3. Run their proposals through evaluate.py with the combined corpus
4. audit.py generates the report from the evaluation outputs
5. Nothing records to OUR chain — outputs go to a client-specific directory

**GaaS (ongoing):**
1. Same as audit, but recurring
2. Client's proposals evaluated monthly/quarterly
3. Results delivered as reports
4. Optional: deploy a contract instance for their on-chain recording
